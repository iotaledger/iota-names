Rising Phoenix 2 Ltd is the controller responsible for the processing of personal data obtained through the Website. We are committed to handling your information with transparency and in compliance with the UK Data Protection Act 2018 and, where applicable, the EU General Data Protection Regulation (“GDPR”).

We operate the Website using cloud-based servers located within the European Union. Where data is transferred or stored outside the EU/EEA or the UK, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with Articles 44–49 GDPR, such as Standard Contractual Clauses and the UK Addendum.

This Policy may be amended or updated from time to time to reflect changes in technology, legal requirements, or our operations. The latest version will always be available on our Website. Your continued use of the Website after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated Policy.

**2\. General Principles of Data Processing**

We process personal data only to the extent necessary to provide a functional, secure, and user-friendly Website. Processing activities are carried out only with your consent or when otherwise permitted by law. The applicable legal bases for processing include Article 6(1)(a) GDPR (consent), Article 6(1)(b) GDPR (performance of a contract or pre-contractual steps), Article 6(1)(c) GDPR (legal obligations), and Article 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interests, particularly in ensuring the stability and security of our systems).

Personal data is retained only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected or as required by applicable law. Once the relevant purpose ceases to apply, or statutory retention periods expire, the data will be deleted or anonymised.

**3\. Processing Activities**

### **3.1 Website Access and Technical Data**

When you access the IOTA Names Website, certain technical information is automatically transmitted by your browser or device and temporarily stored in server log files. This information may include your IP address (stored only temporarily and, where possible, anonymised), browser type and version, operating system, referrer URL, date, and time of access.

The processing of this data is technically necessary to enable the display of the Website, ensure system stability and security, and optimize our online presence. The legal basis for this processing is Article 6(1)(f) GDPR, which permits processing based on our legitimate interests in maintaining the security and functionality of the Website.

The collected log data is not combined with other data sources or used to create user profiles. Log files are automatically deleted after a period of seven (7) days, unless longer retention is necessary to investigate potential security incidents.

### **3.2 Purchase of an IOTA Name**

If you choose to purchase a name through IOTA Names, the transaction is executed directly on the IOTA blockchain via your connected wallet. In the course of such a transaction, the blockchain records information including your wallet address, the registered name, and associated transaction metadata. These records are permanently and publicly stored on the decentralised IOTA network and are visible to all participants.

We do not collect, store, or otherwise process your wallet address, private keys, or payment information. We have no influence over or control of on-chain data processing, as this is determined by the decentralised network itself. For this reason, we are not considered the controller for data recorded on the blockchain.

Our limited role in facilitating user-initiated transactions through the Website interface constitutes processing necessary for the performance of the service requested by the user in accordance with Article 6(1)(b) GDPR.

### **3.3 Contact by Email**

You may contact us directly via the email address provided on the Website. When you do so, we process the personal data you voluntarily include in your communication, such as your email address and any information contained within the message (for example, your name, contact details, or signature).

The purpose of processing this information is to handle your inquiry and respond to your message. The processing is carried out on the basis of Article 6(1)(b) GDPR, where it relates to pre-contractual or contractual communications, or Article 6(1)(f) GDPR, representing our legitimate interest in maintaining effective correspondence.

We retain your message and related correspondence for up to twelve (12) months after your request has been resolved, unless legal obligations require a longer retention period.

### **3.4 Cookies and Analytics (Amplitude)**

The IOTA Names Website uses cookies to ensure its proper functionality and, with your consent, to analyse user behaviour for the purpose of improving our services. Cookies are small text files stored on your device that allow us or our service providers to recognise your browser during subsequent visits.

We use essential cookies that are necessary for the basic operation of the Website and do not require consent. In addition, we use analytical cookies provided by Amplitude, Inc., 201 Third Street, Suite 200, San Francisco, CA 94103, USA (“Amplitude”), which are activated only after you have granted your explicit consent through the cookie banner displayed when you first visit the Website.

Amplitude uses cookies and similar technologies to collect pseudonymised data about your interaction with the Website, such as your browser type, operating system, preferred language, country, city, and general device information (for example, whether you use Windows or macOS). No IP addresses, wallet addresses, or other directly identifying information are collected. The data is aggregated and made available to us solely in statistical form to help us understand how the Website is used and to improve its performance.

The use of Amplitude analytics is based on your consent pursuant to Article 6(1)(a) GDPR. You can withdraw this consent at any time via the “Cookie Settings” link available in the Website footer. Analytics data is retained for a period of up to twelve (12) months before being deleted or fully anonymised.

Where Amplitude processes data outside the United Kingdom or the European Economic Area, such transfers are protected by the Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission and the UK Addendum, ensuring an adequate level of protection for your data.

For more details on how Amplitude processes personal data, please refer to <span> <a  className="font-medium underline"  href="https://amplitude.com/privacy"   target="_blank"   rel="noopener noreferrer"> Amplitude’s Privacy Policy </a>. </span>

If you access or interact with the IOTA Names Service directly through the blockchain without using the Website interface, no cookies are set, and no analytics data is collected.

### **3.5 Session Replay (Amplitude)**

With your consent, we use Amplitude’s Session Replay technology to understand how users interact with the Website and to improve its usability and stability. Session Replay records interaction data such as clicks, scroll behaviour, navigation patterns, and general user interface engagement. All fields that may contain personal or identifying information (such as usernames, wallet details, or free-text inputs) are masked by default in accordance with Amplitude’s privacy settings, and no passwords, payment information, or other sensitive data are collected.

We receive session-level recordings of user interactions; however, due to the applied masking and configuration, the data does not allow us to identify individual users. Any insights derived from these recordings are evaluated exclusively on an aggregated and statistical basis for the purposes of diagnosing usability issues, identifying technical errors, and optimising the Website.

The use of Session Replay is based on your consent pursuant to Article 6(1)(a) GDPR, which you may withdraw at any time via the “Cookie Settings” link on the Website. Data processed via Session Replay is retained for a maximum period of thirty (30) days before being deleted or anonymised.

Where data is processed outside the United Kingdom or the European Economic Area, such transfers are safeguarded through the use of the Standard Contractual Clauses together with the UK Addendum.

### **3.6 Error and Performance Monitoring (Sentry)**

We use Sentry, a monitoring service provided by Functional Software, Inc., to detect and fix technical errors and ensure the stability of the Website. In the event of an error, Sentry records limited technical information such as browser type, operating system, time of occurrence, URL path, and diagnostic details (for example, stack traces).

Sentry does not collect personal data, and the information processed generally does not allow identification of users. However, in rare cases, an indirect link to an individual cannot be fully excluded.

**4\. Your Rights**

As a user, you have several rights concerning the processing of your personal data under the GDPR. These include the right to access the personal data we process about you (Article 15 GDPR), the right to rectify inaccurate data (Article 16 GDPR), and the right to request the erasure of your data under the conditions set out in Article 17 GDPR. You may also request the restriction of processing in accordance with Article 18 GDPR or exercise your right to data portability pursuant to Article 20 GDPR.

Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw this consent at any time with future effect. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before the withdrawal.

You also have the right to object to processing based on our legitimate interests under Article 21 GDPR. Please note that due to the immutable nature of blockchain technology, we cannot modify or delete data recorded on the IOTA network. In such cases, we will take reasonable steps to minimise any potential link between such data and an identifiable person.

To exercise any of your rights, you may contact us at privacy@iota.org. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority if you believe that the processing of your personal data violates applicable law.

**5\. Data Security**

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, misuse, loss, or destruction. These measures include encryption, restricted access controls, and regular security monitoring. However, due to the decentralised and public nature of blockchain networks, we cannot guarantee the deletion or modification of data once it has been recorded on-chain. Users are therefore advised not to link personal data to blockchain addresses or .iota names.

**6\. Changes to this Privacy Policy**

We may update this Policy from time to time to reflect legal, technical, or operational developments. The current version is always available on our Website. Continued use of the Website after updates signifies your acceptance of the revised Policy.
